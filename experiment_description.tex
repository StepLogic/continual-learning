\section{Evaluation Environment}

We evaluate continual RL methods on the \textbf{MetaWorld ML10 benchmark}, a suite of 10 robotic manipulation tasks using a Sawyer arm. The agent must sequentially learn each task and retain knowledge across task switches.

\subsection{Task Sequence}

\begin{table}[h]
\centering
\caption{MetaWorld ML10 task sequence. Each task is trained for 400k steps before switching.}
\label{tab:tasks}
\begin{tabular}{cl}
\toprule
Task & Description \\
\midrule
1 & \texttt{reach-v3} --- Reach target position \\
2 & \texttt{push-v3} --- Push object to goal \\
3 & \texttt{pick-place-v3} --- Pick and place object \\
4 & \texttt{door-open-v3} --- Open door by handle \\
5 & \texttt{drawer-close-v3} --- Close drawer \\
6 & \texttt{button-press-topdown-v3} --- Press button from above \\
7 & \texttt{peg-insert-side-v3} --- Insert peg in side slot \\
8 & \texttt{window-open-v3} --- Open sliding window \\
9 & \texttt{sweep-v3} --- Sweep object off table \\
10 & \texttt{basketball-v3} --- Put ball in hoop \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

\subsection{Environment Details}

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Observation}: 39-dimensional state vector (end-effector position, velocity, gripper state, object/target positions)
    \item \textbf{Action}: 4-dimensional continuous control (end-effector XYZ + gripper)
    \item \textbf{Steps per task}: 400,000
    \item \textbf{Total training}: 4,000,000 steps (4 full cycles through all tasks)
    \item \textbf{Evaluation}: Every 50k steps, 10 episodes per task
\end{itemize}

\section{Baselines}

We compare the following continual RL methods:

\begin{table}[h]
\centering
\caption{Baseline methods evaluated on MetaWorld ML10.}
\label{tab:baselines}
\begin{tabular}{ll}
\toprule
Method & Description \\
\midrule
\textbf{SAC (Base)} & Standard Soft Actor-Critic \\
\textbf{Fast TRAC} & Parameter-free optimizer with gradient clipping \\
\textbf{Parseval} & Orthogonality-regularized networks \\
\textbf{Dual-Learner} & Fast/slow knowledge integration \\
\textbf{World Model} & MPC planning with learned dynamics \\
\textbf{Dual Actor} & Current + distilled actor architecture \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

All methods share identical hyperparameters where applicable: learning rate $3 \times 10^{-4}$, batch size 256, replay buffer 1M transitions, $\gamma=0.99$, $\tau=0.005$.
